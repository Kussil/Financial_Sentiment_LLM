Item 7.01    Regulation FD Disclosure.

I.    Price Matters Update

Including the impact of EOG's crude oil financial derivative contracts and based on EOG's tax position, EOG's price sensitivity as of September 30, 2023, for each $1.00 per barrel increase or decrease in wellhead crude oil and condensate price, combined with the estimated change in NGL price, is approximately $135 million for net income and $173 million for pretax cash flows from operating activities, in each case for the full-year 2023.

Including the impact of EOG's natural gas financial derivative contracts and based on EOG's tax position and the portion of EOG's anticipated natural gas volumes for which prices have not (as of September 30, 2023) been determined under long-term marketing contracts, EOG's price sensitivity as of September 30, 2023, for each $0.10 per thousand cubic feet increase or decrease in wellhead natural gas price, is approximately $33 million for net income and $43 million for pretax cash flows from operating activities, in each case for the full-year 2023.

II.    Price Risk Management

With the objective of enhancing the certainty of future revenues and cash flows, from time to time EOG enters into financial price swap, option, swaption, collar and basis swap contracts. EOG accounts for financial commodity derivative contracts using the mark-to-market accounting method.

For the third quarter of 2023, EOG anticipates a net gain of $43 million on the mark-to-market of its financial commodity derivative contracts. During the third quarter of 2023, the net cash received from settlements of financial commodity derivative contracts was $23 million.

Consistent with EOG's customary practice, in calculating Adjusted Net Income (Loss) (Non-GAAP) for the third quarter of 2023, EOG expects to (among other expected adjustments) subtract from reported Net Income (Loss) the anticipated net gain of $43 million on the mark-to-market of its financial commodity derivative contracts and add to reported Net Income (Loss) the $23 million of net cash received from settlements of financial commodity derivative contracts, as presented in the summary below (in millions).
